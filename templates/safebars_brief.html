SafeBARS: CHI 2027 Project Brief

Bias-calibrated synthetic stakeholders for safer pre-fieldwork action research planning. The project studies how AI can help researchers prepare for real community engagement, not replace it.

Concrete Problem

Researchers preparing online fraud prevention or other high-risk digital safety studies need to test interview questions, workshop scripts, consent language, and safety procedures before fieldwork.

Testing rough materials directly with vulnerable participants can be ethically risky. Ordinary LLM persona role-play is uncontrolled and may miss shame, privacy concerns, institutional distrust, family pressure, distress, or participant burden.

CoBRA-Inspired Move

CoBRA shows that natural-language personas are unreliable and that agent behavior can be shaped through measurable bias abstractions.

SafeBARS adapts this idea into research planning: synthetic stakeholders have adjustable risk-response profiles.

privacy sensitivity shame/stigma sensitivity institutional distrust help-seeking reluctance distress sensitivity

System Workflow

1. PlanResearcher enters initial study plan.
2. StakeholderSelect affected participant, family helper, or community worker.
3. ProfileAdjust bias/risk-response sliders.
4. RehearseChat about interview, workshop, consent, or safety plan.
5. ReflectReport flags risks with evidence notes.
6. ReviseSave revised plan for pre/post analysis.

Prototype Status

Open Prototype

Demo Example

Initial researcher question:

How much money did you lose and what mistake did you make?

Synthetic affected participant may respond:

This wording feels uncomfortable because it sounds like you already think I did something wrong.

Reflection report flags possible victim-blaming, privacy concern, distress risk, and need for skip/pause options.

Key boundary: this is not evidence about real participants. It is a rehearsal signal that should be verified with real participants or community partners.

Proposed Formative Study

  • Participants: HCI/UX/AI safety/design research students or researchers.
  • Task: draft initial action research plan.
  • Use SafeBARS to rehearse with synthetic stakeholders.
  • Generate reflection report and revise plan.
  • Interview about usefulness, risks, and trust calibration.

Possible CHI Contribution

  • Problem framing: synthetic stakeholders as rehearsal aids, not participant replacements.
  • System: bias-aware pre-fieldwork research scaffold.
  • Study: how researchers identify risks and revise plans.
  • Design implications for responsible AI-assisted research preparation.
