## Study Title

SafeBARS: Safe Bias-Aware Action Research Scaffolding with Synthetic Stakeholders

## Study Purpose

This study explores a prototype tool that helps researchers rehearse sensitive action research plans before fieldwork. The tool uses fictional synthetic stakeholders to help researchers notice possible ethical risks, safety gaps, missing stakeholders, and participant burden.

The study evaluates the prototype and research-planning workflow. It does not evaluate your personal research ability.

## Why You Are Invited

You are invited because you have experience or training in HCI, UX research, design research, AI safety, CSCW, or a related area.

## What You Will Do

The session will take about 60 minutes.

You will be asked to:

1. Read a short research-planning scenario.
2. Draft an initial action research plan.
3. Use the SafeBARS prototype to rehearse the plan with fictional synthetic stakeholders.
4. Revise your research plan.
5. Answer interview questions about the prototype and your experience.

## Important Note About Synthetic Stakeholders

The synthetic stakeholders in this prototype are fictional rehearsal aids. They do not represent real participants, real communities, or empirical evidence. Their purpose is to help researchers identify assumptions and questions to verify with real participants or community partners.

## Voluntary Participation

Your participation is voluntary.

You may:

- Refuse to answer any question.
- Pause or stop the session at any time.
- Ask for your data to be removed, subject to the limits described by the final ethics protocol.

## Potential Risks

The risks are expected to be low.

Possible discomforts include:

- Discussing sensitive research topics such as online fraud, privacy, or participant distress.
- Feeling judged about research-planning decisions.

To reduce risk:

- The task uses fictional scenarios.
- You do not need to share personal experiences with fraud or harm.
- The study evaluates the prototype, not you.
- You may skip questions or stop at any time.

## Potential Benefits

There may be no direct personal benefit. Your participation may help improve tools for safer and more reflective research planning.

## Data Collected

The research team may collect:

- Your initial research plan.
- Your interaction logs with the prototype.
- The prototype's reflection report.
- Your revised research plan.
- Interview notes or transcript.
- Optional audio/screen recording, only if separately agreed.

## Data Not Requested

Please do not include:

- Real names of fraud victims or vulnerable people.
- Personal financial information.
- Sensitive personal stories that are not needed for the task.
- Identifiable information about third parties.

## Confidentiality

Your data will be anonymized or pseudonymized for analysis. Reports and publications will not use your real name.

Direct quotes may be used in publications only after removing identifying details.

## Data Storage

Study data will be stored securely according to the final approved data management plan.
