# One-Page Novelty Memo

Last updated: 2026-06-16

## Proposed Project

SafeBARS: Safe Bias-Aware Action Research Scaffolding with Bias-Calibrated Synthetic Stakeholders

## Core Novelty Claim

SafeBARS reframes synthetic stakeholders from participant replacements into controllable rehearsal aids for high-risk action research. Inspired by CoBRA, it makes stakeholder risk-response patterns explicit and adjustable so researchers can inspect assumptions, stress-test study plans, and calibrate trust before engaging real participants.

## Why This Is Not CoBRA

CoBRA contributes a mechanism for measuring and controlling cognitive bias in LLM social agents. SafeBARS contributes a human-AI research scaffold that uses controlled synthetic stakeholder profiles to support researcher reflexivity and safety planning.

CoBRA asks:

Can we control bias in LLM social agents?

SafeBARS asks:

Can controlled stakeholder risk-response profiles help researchers prepare safer and more reflexive fieldwork?

## Why This Is Not Synthetic Participants

SafeBARS does not claim synthetic stakeholders represent real people or communities. It treats them as rehearsal lenses. Every output should include limitation reminders and questions to verify with real participants.

The goal is not:

"What would real participants say?"

The goal is:

"What assumptions and risks should researchers notice before asking real participants?"

## Why This Is Not Anti-Fraud Education

Online fraud is an initial high-risk test domain, but the user is the researcher, not the older adult learner. Prior work such as ROLESafe studies role-based fraud education for older adults. SafeBARS studies AI-assisted preparation for researchers designing such interventions.

## Why This Is Feasible in 1-2 Months

The first version can use prompt-based stakeholder profiles, not full model fine-tuning or activation steering.

Feasible deliverables:

- Working researcher-facing prototype.
- Bias/risk-response profile controls.
- 4-6 stakeholder roles.
- Reflection dashboard.
- Formative study with 6-8 researchers.
- Optional pilot comparison with ordinary LLM brainstorming.

## What CHI Reviewers Should Care About

SafeBARS addresses a growing problem: researchers are likely to use LLMs for early planning and stakeholder role-play, but current tools do not help them understand the limits, risks, or ethical boundaries of doing so.

The contribution is not only the system. The contribution is a design stance:

AI can help researchers prepare for better human engagement, but should not replace that engagement.

## Recommended Paper Framing

Primary framing:

Reflexive pre-fieldwork rehearsal scaffold.

Secondary framing:

Trust-calibrated use of synthetic stakeholders.

Technical inspiration:

CoBRA-style bias control, adapted into stakeholder risk-response profiles.

## Recommended Research Questions

RQ1. How can bias-calibrated synthetic stakeholders scaffold researchers' preparation for high-risk action research?

RQ2. What assumptions, missing stakeholders, ethical risks, and safety gaps do researchers surface through SafeBARS rehearsal?

RQ3. How do explicit bias profiles and limitation-aware reflection reports shape researchers' trust calibration toward synthetic stakeholder outputs?
