# Pre/Post Plan Coding Framework v0

Last updated: 2026-06-17

## Purpose

This framework supports analysis of how participants revise their research plans after using SafeBARS.

The goal is to identify whether SafeBARS helps researchers surface ethical risks, safety gaps, missing stakeholders, hidden assumptions, and trust-calibration needs before fieldwork.

## Unit of Analysis

Primary unit:

- One participant's initial plan and revised plan pair.

Secondary units:

- Specific sentence-level changes.
- Reflection report items.
- Interview explanations for revisions.

## Data Sources

For each participant:

- Initial research plan.
- SafeBARS interaction log.
- Reflection report.
- Revised research plan.
- Interview notes or transcript.

## Coding Categories

### C1. Stakeholder Coverage

Definition:

The revised plan adds or more clearly considers stakeholders who were absent or under-specified in the initial plan.

Examples:

- Adds family helpers or caregivers.
- Adds community workers or local partners.
- Adds platform, bank, police, or authority stakeholders.
- Adds skeptical or disengaged participants.

Code values:

- 0: no change.
- 1: vague mention of additional stakeholders.
- 2: specific additional stakeholder and role.
- 3: stakeholder added with concrete engagement or safety plan.

### C2. Privacy and Data Handling

Definition:

The revised plan adds clearer explanation of privacy, recording, anonymization, data access, data storage, or disclosure boundaries.

Examples:

- Adds recording choices.
- Adds anonymization explanation.
- Explains who will see data.
- Reduces unnecessary financial details.

Code values:

- 0: no privacy/data handling change.
- 1: vague privacy mention.
- 2: concrete privacy or data-use explanation.
- 3: concrete privacy plan plus participant choice or minimization.

### C3. Distress and Safety Protocol

Definition:

The revised plan adds procedures for discomfort, distress, pausing, skipping questions, withdrawal, or support resources.

Examples:

- Adds skip option.
- Adds pause option.
- Adds withdrawal reminder.
- Adds support resources or follow-up.
- Adds private participation option.

Code values:

- 0: no safety protocol change.
- 1: vague mention of participant comfort.
- 2: concrete skip/pause/withdrawal option.
- 3: concrete safety plan plus support/follow-up.

### C4. Victim-Blaming Language Reduction

Definition:

The revised plan removes or softens language that frames harm as the participant's mistake, fault, carelessness, or failure.

Examples:

- Changes "Why did you believe the scam?" to "What made the message seem trustworthy at the time?"
- Changes "What mistake did you make?" to "What information influenced your decision?"
- Adds nonjudgmental framing.

Code values:

- 0: no change.
- 1: minor wording change.
- 2: clear removal of blaming language.
- 3: clear removal plus explicit trauma-informed or nonjudgmental framing.

### C5. Participant Burden Reduction

Definition:

The revised plan reduces time, cognitive load, emotional burden, disclosure burden, or logistical difficulty.

Examples:

- Shortens a workshop or interview.
- Adds breaks.
- Offers alternative participation modes.
- Makes sensitive disclosure optional.
- Reduces group-sharing pressure.

Code values:

- 0: no burden change.
- 1: vague mention of reducing burden.
- 2: concrete burden reduction.
- 3: concrete burden reduction plus accessibility or alternative format.

### C6. Consent Clarity

Definition:

The revised plan clarifies consent, voluntary participation, recording choices, ability to skip, and withdrawal.

Examples:

- Adds plain-language consent script.
- Adds "you can skip any question."
- Adds "you can stop at any time."
- Adds separate consent for recording.

Code values:

- 0: no consent change.
- 1: vague consent mention.
- 2: concrete consent language.
- 3: concrete consent language plus multiple participant choices.

### C7. Community Benefit and Follow-Up

Definition:

The revised plan adds benefit to participants/community or plans what happens after the session.

Examples:

- Adds take-home resource.
- Adds referral pathway.
- Adds community partner feedback.
- Adds follow-up workshop or support.
- Explains what the community receives.

Code values:

- 0: no benefit/follow-up change.
- 1: vague benefit mention.
- 2: concrete resource or follow-up.
- 3: concrete benefit co-designed or reviewed with community partner.

### C8. Trust Calibration About Synthetic Stakeholders

Definition:

The revised plan or interview indicates the participant understands SafeBARS outputs as rehearsal prompts rather than participant evidence.

Examples:

- Notes "must verify with real participants."
- Adds question for community partner review.
- Explicitly says synthetic response is not representative.
- Uses SafeBARS to identify questions, not conclusions.

Code values:

- 0: no trust-calibration evidence.
- 1: vague uncertainty about AI output.
- 2: explicitly notes need to verify with real participants.
- 3: integrates verification step into revised research plan.

### C9. Method Feasibility

Definition:

The revised plan improves practical feasibility of recruitment, session flow, timing, staffing, language, accessibility, or location.

Examples:

- Adds shorter sessions.
- Adds language support.
- Adds staff role.
- Adds accessible scheduling.
- Adds recruitment through community partner.

Code values:

- 0: no feasibility change.
- 1: vague feasibility mention.
- 2: concrete feasibility change.
- 3: concrete feasibility change plus responsible stakeholder/owner.

## Overall Revision Quality

After coding individual categories, assign an overall qualitative rating:

- 0: no meaningful revision.
- 1: superficial revision.
- 2: meaningful revision in one or two areas.
- 3: substantial revision across multiple ethical/safety dimensions.
- 4: substantial revision plus clear plan for real stakeholder verification.

## Coding Procedure

1. Read initial plan.
2. Read revised plan.
3. Highlight additions, deletions, and wording changes.
4. Code each category C1-C9.
5. Note evidence quote from initial and revised plan.
6. Cross-check with SafeBARS reflection report.
7. Cross-check with interview explanation.
8. Assign overall revision quality.

## Reliability Plan

For early formative analysis:

- One primary coder codes all cases.
- A second reviewer checks 25-30% of cases if available.
- Resolve disagreements through discussion.

For CHI paper:

- Report coding process transparently.
- Avoid overclaiming statistical generality.
- Use coding to support qualitative findings.

## Example

Initial:

"We will ask participants how much money they lost and why they believed the message."

Revised:

"We will ask what made the message seem trustworthy at the time. Participants can skip any question, do not need to disclose exact financial loss, and will be told how recordings and notes are stored."

Codes:

- C2 Privacy and Data Handling: 3
- C3 Distress and Safety Protocol: 2
- C4 Victim-Blaming Language Reduction: 3
- C6 Consent Clarity: 2
- Overall Revision Quality: 3
