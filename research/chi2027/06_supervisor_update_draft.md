I have started the first stage of preparing the project for CHI 2027. Based on our previous discussion and the CoBRA paper, I am proposing to reframe the project from an end-user anti-fraud education tool into a researcher-facing action research scaffold.

The tentative project title is:

SafeBARS: Safe Bias-Aware Action Research Scaffolding with Bias-Calibrated Synthetic Stakeholders.

The core problem is that researchers increasingly use LLMs to brainstorm or simulate stakeholders during early-stage study planning, especially in sensitive domains such as online fraud, AI safety, and digital literacy. However, ordinary LLM personas are hard to control, may reproduce stereotypes, and may give researchers false confidence before they work with real participants.

Inspired by CoBRA, I want to explore whether stakeholder risk-response patterns can become measurable and adjustable abstractions. The goal is not to replace real participants. Instead, the system would help researchers rehearse interview protocols, intervention ideas, consent language, and safety procedures before fieldwork.

The tentative research question is:

How can bias-calibrated synthetic stakeholders help researchers prepare safer, more reflexive, and more accountable action research in high-risk digital safety contexts?
