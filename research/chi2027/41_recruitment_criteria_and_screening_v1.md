# SafeBARS Recruitment Criteria and Screening v1

Last updated: 2026-06-17

Status: draft for supervisor approval.

## Study Purpose

This formative study evaluates SafeBARS as a research-planning scaffold. Participants will act as researchers preparing sensitive action research, not as affected community members.

## Target Sample

Planned sample:

- 6-8 participants.

Primary participant profile:

- HCI, UX research, design research, CSCW, AI safety, usable security, or responsible AI students/researchers.
- People who have planned or learned about interviews, workshops, participatory design, user studies, fieldwork, or ethics review.

Preferred diversity within the small sample:

- Different research experience levels, such as master's students, PhD students, research assistants, UX researchers, or design researchers.
- At least 2 participants with prior interview/workshop planning experience.
- At least 2 participants who have used LLMs for brainstorming, role-play, research planning, or writing.
- If possible, 1-2 participants with digital safety, fraud, phishing, privacy, or vulnerable-community research exposure.

## Inclusion Criteria

Participants should:

- be 18 or older;
- be comfortable reading and discussing research-planning materials in English;
- have experience or training in HCI, UX, design research, AI safety, CSCW, responsible AI, usable security, social computing, or a related area;
- be willing to draft and revise a short fictional research plan;
- be willing to use a local prototype and answer interview questions about the experience.

## Exclusion Criteria

For this first formative study, avoid recruiting:

- people as representatives of older adults, fraud victims, or vulnerable communities;
- people who would need to share personal experiences of fraud, financial harm, or scams;
- people currently experiencing distress related to online fraud or financial loss;
- people who may feel evaluated by the researcher, such as direct supervisees, unless the supervisor approves a low-pressure recruitment process;
- people who cannot participate without sharing sensitive personal information.

## Recruitment Channels

Possible channels:

- classmates or labmates with HCI/UX/design research background;
- research students in HCI, CSCW, AI safety, responsible AI, usable security, or social computing;
- university research groups;
- personal academic network;
- supervisor-recommended participants.

Avoid:

- older adult community groups;
- fraud victim support groups;
- public recruitment aimed at people with direct scam experience.

## Screening Questions

Use these before scheduling:

1. Are you 18 or older?
2. What is your current role or background?
3. Do you have experience or training in user research, design research, HCI, CSCW, AI safety, responsible AI, usable security, or a related area?
4. Have you planned or participated in interviews, workshops, user studies, fieldwork, or ethics review before?
5. Have you used LLMs for brainstorming, role-play, research planning, writing, or similar tasks?
6. Are you comfortable discussing a fictional online-fraud-prevention research scenario without sharing personal fraud experiences?
7. Are you comfortable with a 60-minute session that may include prototype use, plan revision, and interview questions?

## Participant Grouping for Analysis

Record, without identifying details:

- participant ID, such as P01;
- role category, such as master's student, PhD student, UX researcher;
- research experience level, such as low, medium, high;
- LLM use experience, such as none, occasional, frequent;
- prior sensitive-fieldwork experience, yes/no/brief note;
- prior digital-safety or fraud-related research exposure, yes/no/brief note.

## Recruitment Message

Short version:

> Hi, I am recruiting participants for a 60-minute formative study of SafeBARS, a prototype that helps researchers rehearse sensitive action research plans with fictional synthetic stakeholders before fieldwork. The study is about research planning, not personal fraud experiences. You would draft a short research plan, try the prototype, revise the plan, and answer interview questions. I am looking for people with HCI, UX research, design research, AI safety, CSCW, usable security, or related experience/training. Please let me know if you are interested.

## Scheduling Checklist

Before confirming:

- participant meets inclusion criteria;
- participant understands no personal fraud experience is required;
- session language is confirmed;
- time and location/meeting link are confirmed;
- consent/information sheet is sent if required;
- participant ID is assigned.

## Recruitment Stop Rule

Stop after 6 participants if:

- the study already surfaces repeated usability and research-planning themes;
- the supervisor agrees the formative stage is sufficient.

Recruit up to 8 participants if:

- early sessions show high variation;
- additional experience profiles are needed;
- the prototype or study task changes after the first 2-3 sessions.
