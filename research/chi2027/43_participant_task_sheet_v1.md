# SafeBARS Participant Task Sheet v1

Last updated: 2026-06-17

## Study Task

You will use SafeBARS, a prototype that helps researchers rehearse sensitive research plans with fictional synthetic stakeholders before fieldwork.

The study is about research planning and prototype feedback. You do not need to share any personal experiences with online fraud, scams, or financial harm.

## Important Boundary

Synthetic stakeholders are fictional rehearsal prompts. They do not represent real participants, real older adults, fraud victims, or communities.

Please use their responses to inspect your plan and generate questions for real fieldwork, not as evidence about what real participants would say.

## Scenario

Imagine you are preparing an action research project about online fraud prevention for older adults in a community setting.

You want to prepare interviews or a workshop, but before meeting real participants you need to check whether your questions, consent language, workshop plan, and safety procedures might create problems.

Possible issues include:

- shame or embarrassment;
- privacy and recording concerns;
- participant distress;
- victim-blaming wording;
- family or caregiver pressure;
- institutional distrust;
- participant burden;
- unclear consent or withdrawal options.

## Part 1: Initial Research Plan

Please draft a short initial plan.

You can write bullet points. It does not need to be polished.

### Research goal

What are you trying to learn or support?

### Target community

Who would participate or be affected?

### Planned method

Would you use interviews, workshops, participatory design, surveys, or another method?

### Draft materials

Write 2-4 example questions, activities, or script excerpts you might use.

### Known risks

What ethical, safety, privacy, or emotional risks do you already expect?

### Uncertainties

What are you unsure about before fieldwork?

## Part 2: SafeBARS Rehearsal

Open the prototype:

`http://127.0.0.1:5050/safebars/study`

Steps:

1. Enter your plan into the left panel.
2. Choose a stakeholder preset.
3. Click `Start Session`.
4. Ask the synthetic stakeholder to react to your questions, workshop script, consent language, or safety plan.
5. Ask at least 3 questions.
6. Click `Generate Reflection`.
7. Review the reflection report.

You may ask questions like:

- Does this interview question sound blaming?
- Would this recording plan raise privacy concerns?
- Is this workshop too long or burdensome?
- What might make participants distrust this study?
- What support or follow-up is missing?
- What should I verify with real participants or community partners?

## Part 3: Revised Research Plan

After rehearsal, revise your plan.

Please include:

- what you changed;
- why you changed it;
- what you still need to verify with real participants or community partners.

You may revise:

- question wording;
- consent language;
- recording/data handling;
- safety procedure;
- session length;
- support resources;
- stakeholder involvement;
- follow-up plan.

## Part 4: Discussion

After the task, we will discuss:

- what was useful;
- what felt confusing or risky;
- whether the synthetic stakeholder felt too easy to trust;
- what should be improved;
- whether this tool could help researchers prepare for sensitive fieldwork.

## Reminder

The goal is not to create a perfect plan. The goal is to understand whether SafeBARS helps researchers notice issues before real fieldwork.
