# SafeBARS Literature Matrix v1

Last updated: 2026-06-17

Purpose: build a CHI-facing literature base for SafeBARS. The goal is not to prove that synthetic stakeholders are accurate people. The goal is to justify a bounded, safety-oriented rehearsal scaffold for researchers preparing sensitive action research.

## Reading Legend

- Central: must appear in related work or introduction.
- Support: helps frame domain, method, or system design.
- Threat: reviewers may use this to question novelty.
- Method: useful for study or evaluation design.

## A. Controllable LLM Agents and Social Simulation

### 1. CoBRA: Cognitive Bias Regulator for Social Agents

- Link: https://github.com/AISmithLab/CoBRA
- Paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.13588
- Type: Central / Method inspiration
- Main idea: LLM social agents need measurable and controllable behavioral tendencies rather than only natural-language persona descriptions.
- SafeBARS use: primary inspiration for bias-calibrated stakeholder profiles and behavior regulation.
- Gap for SafeBARS: CoBRA focuses on agent control and benchmarking. SafeBARS focuses on a researcher-facing scaffold for planning safer fieldwork.
- Threat-to-novelty note: we must avoid claiming that our contribution is simply "CoBRA for fraud." Our contribution should be a new HCI workflow: rehearsal, reflection, and trust-calibrated use before real community engagement.

### 2. Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03442
- Type: Central background
- Main idea: LLM agents can produce believable social behavior through memory, reflection, and planning.
- SafeBARS use: establishes that agent-based simulation is technically plausible.
- Gap for SafeBARS: believability is not enough for sensitive research preparation. SafeBARS asks how such agents can be constrained and framed so they help researchers notice risk without replacing participants.

### 3. Lost in Simulation: LLM-Simulated Users are Unreliable Proxies for Human Users in Agentic Evaluations

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17087
- Type: Central caution / Threat
- Main idea: LLM-simulated users are unreliable proxies for real users in agentic evaluation, with robustness and fairness concerns.
- SafeBARS use: strongly supports the "not replacement" boundary.
- Gap for SafeBARS: the paper critiques simulated users as evaluation proxies. SafeBARS studies whether bounded synthetic stakeholders can support pre-fieldwork reflection.

### 4. Large Language Models that Replace Human Participants Can Harmfully Misportray and Flatten Identity Groups

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.01908
- Type: Central caution
- Main idea: replacing human participants with LLMs can flatten identity groups and misrepresent lived experience.
- SafeBARS use: ethical foundation for "synthetic stakeholders are rehearsal materials, not evidence."
- Gap for SafeBARS: provides critique and warning, but not a design pattern for safer pre-fieldwork scaffolding.

### 5. Can AI Replace Human Subjects? A Large-Scale Replication of Psychological Experiments with LLMs

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.00128
- Type: Support / Caution
- Main idea: LLMs may reproduce some aggregate effects but struggle with interactions, uncertainty, and false positives.
- SafeBARS use: supports a careful middle position: useful as an aid, not as a substitute.
- Gap for SafeBARS: psychology experiment replication differs from HCI action research planning.

### 6. Out of One, Many: Using Language Models to Simulate Human Samples

- Link: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/political-analysis/article/out-of-one-many-using-language-models-to-simulate-human-samples/035D7C8A55A8C1DA2C3CBB9FE5B8087F
- Type: Background / Threat
- Main idea: language models can be conditioned to approximate survey responses from demographic groups.
- SafeBARS use: useful background for why synthetic populations are attractive.
- Gap for SafeBARS: survey approximation is not the same as safe rehearsal for fieldwork with vulnerable communities.

### 7. Using Large Language Models to Simulate Multiple Humans and Replicate Human Subject Studies

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.10264
- Type: Background / Threat
- Main idea: LLMs can simulate responses in some human-subject study settings.
- SafeBARS use: positions SafeBARS against a replacement-oriented path.
- Gap for SafeBARS: our research question is not whether LLMs can reproduce human data, but whether they can help researchers inspect plans before collecting human data.

### 8. Can Large Language Models Replace Humans in the Systematic Review Process?

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.17526
- Type: Support / Method caution
- Main idea: LLMs can assist review work but need careful prompts, oversight, and limits.
- SafeBARS use: supports a human-in-the-loop research-assistance framing.
- Gap for SafeBARS: not about synthetic stakeholders, ethics rehearsal, or vulnerable communities.

## B. Participatory AI, Action Research, and Reflexive Preparation

### 9. The Participatory Turn in AI Design: Theoretical Foundations and the Current State of Practice

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00907
- Type: Central
- Main idea: participatory AI varies in how much agency stakeholders actually receive.
- SafeBARS use: grounds the ethical boundary that synthetic stakeholders must prepare for, not replace, real participation.
- Gap for SafeBARS: does not design a pre-fieldwork rehearsal system.

### 10. Enabling Value Sensitive AI Systems through Participatory Design Fictions

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.07381

- Main idea: participatory design fictions can help surface values and tensions around AI systems.
- SafeBARS use: frames synthetic stakeholder interaction as a type of rehearsal fiction or assumption probe.
- Gap for SafeBARS: design fictions are not bias-calibrated LLM stakeholders and do not provide real-time researcher feedback.

### 11. Participatory Design: The Third Space in HCI

- Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1207/S15327051HCI1523_02

- Main idea: participatory design creates a "third space" where designers and participants negotiate meanings and knowledge.
- SafeBARS use: helps argue that fieldwork preparation should make researcher assumptions visible.
- Gap for SafeBARS: not about AI rehearsal tools.

### 12. Participatory Action Research: Communicative Action and the Public Sphere

- Link: https://sk.sagepub.com/books/the-sage-handbook-of-qualitative-research-3e/n23.xml
- Type: Foundational support
- Main idea: participatory action research treats knowledge production as collaborative and action-oriented.
- SafeBARS use: establishes why sensitive community research needs reflexive preparation.
- Gap for SafeBARS: does not address AI-based research scaffolding.

### 13. Review of Community-Based Research: Assessing Partnership Approaches to Improve Public Health

- Link: https://www.annualreviews.org/content/journals/10.1146/annurev.publhealth.19.1.173
- Type: Foundational support
- Main idea: community-based research emphasizes partnership, co-learning, local relevance, and mutual benefit.

- Gap for SafeBARS: not about HCI tools or LLMs.

### 14. Value Sensitive Design and Information Systems

- Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1207/s15327051hci0803_3

- Main idea: technology design should account for human values through conceptual, empirical, and technical investigations.
- SafeBARS use: supports designing profiles around ethical concerns such as privacy, shame, distrust, and distress.
- Gap for SafeBARS: broad framework, not a specific rehearsal method.

### 15. Ethics and Society Review: Ethics Reflection as a Precondition for AI Research

- Gap for SafeBARS: does not provide interactive stakeholder rehearsal.

## C. Digital Safety, Fraud, and Vulnerable Communities

### 16. ROLESafe: Experiencer, Helper, or Observer

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12324
- Type: Threat / Related work
- Main idea: role-based online fraud simulation can improve older adults' fraud awareness.
- SafeBARS use: shows online fraud education is active and close to CHI/HCI concerns.
- Gap for SafeBARS: ROLESafe targets older adult learners. SafeBARS targets researchers preparing study plans.
- Threat-to-novelty note: do not frame SafeBARS as an anti-fraud training app for older adults.

### 17. Intergenerational Support for Deepfake Scams Targeting Older Adults

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.11579

- Main idea: older adults often rely on trusted family or social relationships to interpret deepfake scam risk.
- SafeBARS use: justifies the family-helper stakeholder agent and family-pressure risk dimension.
- Gap for SafeBARS: studies scam resilience, not researcher rehearsal.

### 18. Sixteen Years of Phishing User Studies: What Have We Learned?

- Gap for SafeBARS: not about LLM scaffolding or action research.

## Current Novelty Assessment

The strongest novelty angle is not "LLM simulates vulnerable participants." That is ethically risky and likely crowded.

The stronger CHI angle is:

> How can we design a bounded, bias-aware synthetic stakeholder scaffold that helps researchers rehearse sensitive action research plans, surface ethical/safety assumptions, and calibrate trust before real fieldwork?

This angle is supported by the literature gap:

- Agent simulation work shows plausibility but not enough safety.
- Synthetic participant critique shows replacement is risky.
- Participatory/action research shows real stakeholder agency is necessary.
- Digital safety research shows online fraud work involves sensitive risks.
- Human-AI trust work shows AI tools need explicit trust calibration.
