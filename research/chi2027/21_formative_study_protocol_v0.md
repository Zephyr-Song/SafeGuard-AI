# SafeBARS Formative Study Protocol v0

Last updated: 2026-06-16

## Status

Draft for supervisor discussion. This is not yet an approved ethics/IRB document.

## Study Goal

Understand whether SafeBARS helps researchers identify ethical risks, safety gaps, hidden assumptions, and missing stakeholders before conducting high-risk digital safety action research.

## Study Type

Formative prototype study.

## Tentative Research Questions

RQ1. How do researchers use bias-calibrated synthetic stakeholders to rehearse high-risk action research plans?

RQ2. What ethical risks, missing stakeholders, hidden assumptions, and safety gaps do researchers identify through SafeBARS?

RQ3. How do researchers understand the limits of synthetic stakeholder outputs after using SafeBARS?

## Participants

Target participants:

- HCI, UX, CSCW, AI safety, or design research students/researchers.
- Ideally people with some experience planning interviews, workshops, or user studies.

Estimated sample:

- 6-8 participants for formative stage.

Inclusion criteria:

- Age 18+.
- Experience or training in user research, design research, HCI, CSCW, AI safety, or related fields.
- Comfortable conducting the session in English or Chinese, depending on study setup.

Exclusion criteria:

- People currently experiencing distress related to online fraud or financial harm, if the session uses fraud-related scenarios.
- People who are direct intended beneficiaries of the future fraud-prevention intervention, because this first study is about researcher planning rather than affected participants.

## Study Context

The formative task will use online fraud prevention as the initial high-risk digital safety case.

The participant will act as a researcher preparing an action research plan, not as a fraud victim or learner.

## Study Materials

- SafeBARS prototype.
- Pre-task research plan template.
- Post-task revised plan template.
- Interview guide.
- Consent form or study information sheet.
- Optional short background brief about the action research scenario.

## Session Length

Approximate duration:

- 60 minutes.

## Study Procedure

### 1. Introduction and Consent: 5 minutes

Explain:

- The study investigates a prototype for research planning.
- Synthetic stakeholders are fictional rehearsal aids.
- The session is not evaluating the participant's personal skill.
- Participants may skip questions or stop at any time.

### 2. Pre-Task Planning: 10 minutes

Participant receives a scenario:

"You are preparing an action research project about online fraud prevention for older adults or another vulnerable community. You need to plan interviews or a workshop before entering the field."

Participant writes an initial plan:

- Research goal.
- Target community.
- Planned method.
- Draft questions or workshop script.
- Safety concerns.

### 3. Prototype Walkthrough: 5 minutes

Researcher introduces SafeBARS:

- Research plan input.
- Stakeholder preset.
- Bias/risk-response sliders.
- Rehearsal chat.
- Reflection report.
- Revised plan capture.

Emphasize:

- Outputs are not participant evidence.
- The goal is to inspect assumptions and risks.

### 4. Rehearsal Task: 20 minutes

Participant uses SafeBARS to rehearse their plan.

Suggested actions:

- Select an affected participant profile.
- Ask about interview questions.
- Try a family helper or community worker if time allows.
- Generate a reflection report.
- Adjust bias sliders if useful.

Researcher observes:

- What prompts participants try.
- Which stakeholder responses they notice.
- Whether they revise their wording or safety procedures.
- Whether they question the synthetic stakeholder's limits.

### 5. Revised Plan: 10 minutes

Participant revises their research plan based on the rehearsal.

They should note:

- What changed.
- Why it changed.
- What still needs real participant or community partner input.

### 6. Post-Study Interview: 10-15 minutes

Use the interview guide.

Focus:

- What was useful.
- What felt misleading or insufficient.
- What risks were surfaced.
- How the system affected trust in synthetic stakeholders.
- What should be changed before real use.

## Data Collected

- Initial research plan.
- SafeBARS interaction logs.
- Reflection report.
- Revised research plan.
- Interview notes or transcript.
- Optional screen recording, if approved and consented.

## Data to Avoid Collecting

- Real names of affected people.
- Personal fraud experiences from study participants.
- Sensitive financial details.
- Any identifiable information not needed for analysis.

## Potential Risks

Risks are expected to be low because participants are researchers rehearsing a fictional research plan.

Possible risks:

- Mild discomfort discussing fraud, vulnerability, or research ethics.
- Concern about being judged on research planning skill.
- Privacy concern if sessions are recorded.

Mitigation:

- Make clear the study evaluates the prototype, not the participant.
- Allow skipping questions.
- Avoid asking participants to share personal fraud experiences.
- Use fictional scenarios.
- Store data anonymously or pseudonymously.

## Analysis Plan

### Artifact Analysis

Compare initial and revised research plans.

Coding dimensions:

- Added stakeholder roles.
- Added consent/privacy details.
- Added distress handling.
- Removed or softened victim-blaming wording.
- Added follow-up support.
- Added limitation statements about synthetic stakeholders.

### Interaction Log Analysis

Code:

- Types of questions asked to stakeholders.
- Rehearsal signals triggered.
- Bias profile adjustments.
- Moments of trust or skepticism.

### Interview Analysis

Thematic analysis around:

- Perceived usefulness.
- Ethical risk discovery.
- Stakeholder coverage.
- Trust calibration.
- Limits and failure modes.
- Desired improvements.

## Success Indicators

The formative study is promising if participants:

- Identify risks they had not included in the initial plan.
- Revise interview questions or safety procedures.
- Mention missing stakeholders.
- Recognize the limits of synthetic stakeholder rehearsal.
- Suggest concrete prototype improvements.

## Expected Output

- Formative findings for CHI paper.
- Prototype improvement list.
- Refined study design for a later pilot comparison.
